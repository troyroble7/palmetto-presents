Base Power — Competitive Intelligence

Last Updated: February 2026

Company Profile

  • Founded: 2023
  • HQ: Austin, Texas
  • CEO: Zach Dell (Zachary Dell)
  • Valuation: $4 billion (Oct 2025 Series C)
  • Total Funding: ~$1.47 billion ($268M early rounds + $200M Series B + $1B Series C)
  • Employees: ~250 (Oct 2025)
  • Customers: 10,000+ homeowners (Texas only)
  • Deployed Capacity: 150+ MWh residential batteries
  • Install Rate: ~40 systems/day
  • Website: basepowercompany.com

Key Investors

Addition (led Series C), Andreessen Horowitz, Valor Equity Partners, Lennar (homebuilder + investor), CapitalG (Google), Thrive Capital, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Lowercarbon, Altimeter, Trust Ventures

Business Model — "Battery-as-a-Service"

  • NOT a solar company — battery lease + retail electricity
  • Base OWNS the batteries, homeowner leases
  • Vertically integrated: manufacturing, installation, retail electricity, grid services
  • Revenue: retail electricity (8.5¢/kWh fixed, 36-mo contracts) + grid arbitrage (VPP) + ancillary services (ERCOT ADER) + membership fees ($19-29/mo) + utility partnerships

Go-to-Market Channels

  • D2C — online signup, in-house installation crews (no EPC partners)
  • Homebuilder — Lennar partnership (~20 TX communities) — DIRECT THREAT to New Homes
  • Utility — GVEC (2 MW VPP), Farmers Electric Coop, El Paso Electric (Feb 10, 2026): First regulated utility deal. ~1,000 batteries, 10 MW dispatchable. Homeowners get $250 one-time incentive, batteries installed on utility side of meter
  • Manufacturing — In-house at 90K sq ft Austin facility, target 4 GWh/yr capacity. Building new battery factory in downtown Austin (former Statesman printing press site)

Geographic Footprint

  • Current: Texas only (Austin/DFW via Oncor, Houston via CenterPoint, El Paso via EPE)
  • National expansion strategy: Targeting both regulated and deregulated markets. No specific states confirmed for 2026 yet. $1B Series C war chest enables rapid entry

TX Storage Context (Ohm Q3-25)

  • TX residential storage: 70 MWh Q3-25 (+95% YoY) — fast growing but from small base
  • TX storage attach rate: 31% — below national 38% average
  • Base Power validates standalone ESS economics in TX (Ohm noted)
  • National storage forecast: 3.9 GW residential in 2026, 4,013 MWh projected 2025 (+49%)
