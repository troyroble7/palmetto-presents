Residential Battery Sector Intelligence (Feb 2026)

Market Overview

  • Ohm Q3-25: 1,026 MWh deployed Q3-25 (+40% YoY). National attach rate: 38%. 2025 projected: 4,013 MWh (+49%)
  • State attach rates (Ohm Q3-25): HI 92%, CA NEM3 87%, CA 79%, IL 48%, NC 48%, AZ 46%, TX 31%, FL 15%
  • 2026 forecast: 3.9 GW residential storage
  • FEOC/domestic content = gating factor for TPO platforms
  • Tesla PW3 integrated inverter = biggest market disruption since microinverters

Enphase (ENPH) — Full Company Profile

  • Market cap: ~$5.6B | Stock: ~$42 | Cash: $1.51B
  • FY25: $1.47B rev (+10.5% YoY), 46% non-GAAP GM, $172M GAAP net income
  • Revenue declining: Q3 $410M → Q4 $343M → Q1'26 guide $270-300M
  • Headcount: ~2,500 (6% layoff Jan 2026, ~160 people)
  • CEO: Badri Kothandaraman (predicts 20% resi solar market drop in 2026)

Batteries

  • Battery share: ~25% (#2, declining from 29%)
  • Products: IQ Battery 5P (5 kWh), IQ Battery 10C (10 kWh), 5th-gen coming H2 2026 (stackable 5kWh blocks, ~50% higher density, ~40% lower cost)
  • FEOC: Compliant (DOM SKUs only, after 11/10/25 manufacture date)
  • DC Boost: Yes
  • LR AVL: Fully approved (5P-DOM + 10C-DOM), no holdbacks

Inverters (Microinverters)

  • Inverter share (Ohm Q3-25): 37% (#1, down from 73% in 2022 — PW3 disruption). Pricing: $0.435/Wac (-5% QoQ)
  • ADLP price increase: 10-20% on select microinverters effective Feb 9, 2026
  • IQ8 Series: Current gen, 245-384 VA, ~97% efficiency, 25-yr warranty, grid-forming (Sunlight Backup)
  • IQ8HC: Flagship residential (384 VA, DOM SKU: IQ8HC-72-M-DOM-US)
  • IQ9: Next gen GaN technology — 97.5% efficiency, 16A DC input, supports 600W+ panels
  • IQ9N-3P (commercial): Shipping now, 50K+ units ordered Q1
  • IQ9 (residential): Expected Q1-Q2 2026
  • 74% of installers use Enphase — highest penetration of any brand
  • $0.80-1.10/W (vs SolarEdge $0.75-1.00/W, vs Tesla PW3 ~$0.05/W bundled)
  • 25-yr warranty matches lease term (no mid-life replacement vs SolarEdge's 12yr)
  • Safe harbor: $173M+ in agreements with TPO providers (incl. IQ9 resi pre-shipping)

Software

  • Enlighten: Panel-level monitoring, fleet management
  • Solargraf: Design/proposal/permitting — fully integrated with LightReach, EnFin, GoodLeap
  • FEOC Compliance Tool: Serial number-level verification

Manufacturing (US)

  • 6 production lines: SC (Flex), TX (Salcomp), WI — ~80% of shipments from US
  • 4.8-7.2 GWac/yr microinverter capacity
  • DOM SKUs exceed current and future DC thresholds (45%→50%→55%)

Key Risks

  • 69% battery price premium per kWh
  • PW3 integrated inverter capturing share
  • Revenue declining trajectory
  • Propel/Concert (Enphase-only TPO, ~40 installers, 4 states)

Tesla Energy — Full Company Profile

  • Tesla Energy FY25: $12.8B rev (+27% YoY), 29.8% GM — most profitable Tesla division
  • Q4 2025: $3.84B rev (+25% YoY), $1.1B gross profit (record), 28.7% GM
  • Storage deployments: 46.7 GWh in 2025 (+49% YoY), 1M+ cumulative Powerwalls
  • Energy = 23% of Tesla's total profit on just 13% of revenue
  • Revenue is ~90%+ Megapack (utility-scale) — residential solar is tiny and declining
  • Solar installations collapsed: Stopped reporting MW in Q1 2024. Last: 41 MW (Q4 2023, -59% YoY). FY2023: 223 MW (-36%). Share: 16% (SolarCity era) → 1.6% (2024). Not on SPW Top Resi list
  • Headcount: 125,665 total (Dec 2024, -10.5% from 2023). Apr 2024 mass layoff: ~14K people. Senior departures: Drew Baglino (SVP Energy), Rohan Patel (VP Policy)
  • Buffalo GF2: 2,399 FT employees, 3,460+ statewide (finally met NY job req Feb 2026)
  • 48E manufacturing credits: $756M (FY2024)
  • Full competitive analysis: ~/Documents/Brain/Tesla Energy vs Palmetto — Competitive Analysis.md

Batteries

  • Battery share: ~30-47% (#1, but declining on anti-Musk sentiment)
  • PW3 pricing: $8K-$11K hardware, $12K-$16K installed. Expansion ~$6K (~$444/kWh)
  • Powerwall 3: 13.5 kWh, 11.5 kW continuous, LFP, integrated 6-MPPT solar inverter, 10-yr warranty
  • Powerwall 3P: Announced Feb 2026, native 3-phase (EU/AU markets)
  • PW2 recall: 10,500 units (Nov 2025), class action (Brown v. Tesla)
  • "Next Million" Rebate: $500-$1,000 per PW3 (install Jan-Sep 2026, order Nov '25-Mar '26)
  • FEOC: Compliant on LR AVL | DC Boost: Yes
  • LR AVL: Fully approved (2 SKUs), strict install guidelines

Inverter Disruption (via PW3)

  • PW3's integrated 11.5kW/6-MPPT inverter eliminates separate string inverter
  • Effective inverter cost: ~$0.05/W (vs Enphase $0.80-1.10/W, SolarEdge $0.75-1.00/W)
  • Tesla inverter share: ~3% (2022) → ~32% (H1 2025) — 1,100% growth via PW3
  • Enphase share dropped from 73% to ~47-50% because of this

Solar Panels (NEW Jan 2026)

  • TSP-415/TSP-420: US-manufactured (Buffalo, NY), 18 power zones, rail-less mounting
  • 420W, 20.5% efficiency, 71.1" x 44.7" x 1.57", 33% faster install
  • 25-yr warranty, ~300 MW capacity (targeting 1 GW mid-2026)
  • Ecosystem-locked: Designed for Tesla inverter — proprietary lock-in

Solar Lease (Relaunched Oct 2025)

  • 25-year term, 3%/yr escalator, $600 upfront ($100 deposit + $500 progress)
  • Buyout after year 5 at FMV. 95% availability guarantee
  • Direct-only channel — certified installers CANNOT offer the Tesla lease
  • Available in ~28 states for financing. Tesla claims lease available in 41 states + DC
  • Customer reviews: EnergySage 4.3/5 (699 reviews), SolarReviews 2.61/5, ConsumerAffairs 1.8/5, BBB Not Accredited (308+ complaints)

Battery-Only Lease (Launched Oct 2025)

  • 12-year term, $100/PW deposit, 3%/yr escalator, est. $75-100/mo
  • Includes installation, maintenance, monitoring, battery/inverter replacement
  • CRITICAL: NOT a true standalone battery lease — primarily bundled with solar
  • Powerwall Direct (standalone, no solar) = cash/loan only, NOT lease
  • Direct-only channel — certified installers CANNOT offer the Tesla lease
  • Tesla claims ITC as owner, passes through via lower payments

Tesla Electric (TX/ERCOT)

  • Retail electricity provider — dynamic/fixed rate plans
  • $10/mo/PW VPP credit + ~17c/kWh sellback
  • GVEC partnership: ~$2,530 initial incentive + $862.50/yr per PW3
  • Part of ERCOT Aggregated DER pilot

Anti-Musk Sentiment (Quantified)

  • 9-point PW selection drop in 7 weeks post-inauguration (73% → 64%)
  • 20-60% of customers now avoid Tesla products
  • Installer reports: ~50% volume drops, switching to FranklinWH
  • 68% of Tesla mentions on EnergySage = requests for alternatives
  • Concentrated in blue-leaning suburban markets (core solar demo)
  • Brand value lost $15.4B (-36%) in 2025 — now $27.6B, down from $66.2B peak (Jan 2023)
  • US recommendation score fell to 4/10 (from 8+ in 2023)
  • Customers switching to FranklinWH despite $2K higher price
  • "Tesla Takedown" grassroots boycott movement — protests at stores globally

VPP

  • 250K+ Powerwalls enrolled in VPP, $9.9M paid in 2024
  • 535 MW delivered to CA grid (Jul 2025)
  • Closed Autobidder ecosystem — rec = "low priority"

Channel Model (Shifting)

  • Sep 2024: Formally called for third-party installers to get certified — exiting direct installation
  • ~2,000 certified installers across 14+ countries for hardware (PW3, panels, Solar Roof)
  • Certified installers sell hardware (cash/loan) but CANNOT offer Tesla lease
  • Tesla lease = direct-only. No ABS program. No EPC financing channel
  • Controlled allocation, no open distribution, 4-7 month waits
  • Tesla solar installation share: 1.6% (effectively exited installation)

FranklinWH

  • Market share: Mid-single digits (#3-5, rising fast)
  • Products: aPower 2 (15 kWh/10 kW), aPower S (15 kWh + 11.5 kW inverter), aGate controller
  • Company: Private, Series B, $25M funding, ~100 employees, 3,000+ installers
  • FEOC: On AVL (implied compliant) but cell origin NOT disclosed (likely Chinese)
  • DC Boost: NOT eligible
  • LR AVL: Pre-approval required, allocations FULL (most restrictive)
  • VPP: Best VPP-ready option (EnergyHub + Leap), first to support both SRP + APS
  • Strategic priority: Expand allocations (Priority #4, Q1 2026 roadmap), press for FEOC transparency

Lunar Energy

  • Market share (Ohm Q3-25): ~1% among CA NEM3 projects — first measurable share. <1% nationally (~2,000 systems deployed, primarily CA)
  • Products: Lunar System (10-30 kWh modular, 9.6 kW, 95.5% RTE)
  • Company: Private, $532M+ funding, ~$1B valuation, CEO ex-Tesla Energy
  • FEOC: Non-FEOC (US/European cells, GA manufacturing)
  • DC Boost: Not assessed (not on standard AVL)
  • Gridshare: Device-agnostic DERMS managing 130K+ devices, powers all Sunrun VPPs
  • Sunrun entanglement: Investor + board chair + largest customer = strategic tension
  • Scale roadmap: 10K → 20K (2026) → 100K (2028) systems/yr

SolarEdge Battery (see solaredge-market-intel.md)

  • Energy Bank (9.7 kWh, 94.5% RTE), Nexis coming H2 2026
  • On AVL (1 SKU), FEOC compliant, DC Boost eligible
  • Closed energy storage division (Nov 2024), now uses third-party cells
  • Ohm Q3-25: ~15% storage OEM share, 22% inverter share (#3 behind Enphase 37% and Tesla 29%)
