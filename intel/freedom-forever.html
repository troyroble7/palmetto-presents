Freedom Forever — Competitive Risk Assessment

RISK LEVEL: HIGH

Date: March 5, 2026

Executive Summary

Freedom Forever is the #2 residential solar installer in the US behind Sunrun — but is in full contraction mode. They have abandoned 10 of 30 state markets, laid off ~20% of employees (Feb 2026), carry a BBB C+ rating with a "Pattern of Complaints" alert, are on CSLB probation in California, and have confirmed subcontractor payment problems including mechanics liens on homeowners' properties. Their heavy reliance on Mosaic for loan financing collapsed when Mosaic filed Chapter 11 in June 2025, forcing a scramble to pivot to TPO financing with 4-5 TPO providers.

Freedom Forever is NOT a financing competitor — they are an EPC installer. They partner with GoodLeap, EnFin, EverBright, and other financing providers for TPO and loan products.

2. Financial Distress Indicators

Market Exit & Layoffs (Feb 2026)

  • Abandoned 10 of 30 state markets — 33% geographic footprint reduction
  • Laid off ~20% of employees (confirmed by Ben Airth to Reuters, Feb 5, 2026)
  • Pivoting from 75% cash/loan to majority TPO financing
  • ~500 employees in Florida alone — FL is a key market but high-delinquency state for solar loans
  • Strategy: "Home in on only the most attractive markets" — survival mode, not growth mode

25D ITC Expiration Impact (Jan 2026)

  • The 30% federal homeowner tax credit (25D) expired Dec 31, 2025 under OBBBA
  • Ohm Analytics expects residential installs to decline 20% in 2026 (vs prior 8% growth forecast)
  • Freedom Forever pivoting to TPO/lease models to access 48E commercial ITC — launched third-party ownership products with Enphase
  • Solar Power World (Jan 2026): "Solar layoffs and closures run rampant in Trump's first year back" — FF among companies highlighted

Executive Language (Jun 2025 — Semafor)

  • Ben Airth: Company may be forced to "shrink back to the size of a mom and pop" if ITC eliminated
  • COO Brian Eglsaer: "If it's not [preserved], you're going to see massive layoffs. You'll see lots of companies go out of business."
  • These are survival-mode statements from a company claiming to be the #2 installer in America

Bankruptcy Risk Assessment

  • Freedom Forever has NOT filed for bankruptcy as of Feb 2026
  • However: 10 state exits + 20% layoffs + Mosaic collapse + subcontractor payment problems + CSLB probation = company in defensive survival mode
  • Industry context: Purelight Power (Ch.11 Dec 2025), TriSMART (ceased ops), Mosaic (Ch.11 Jun 2025), Sunnova (Ch.11 Jun 2025)
  • Private company — no public financial filings. Actual debt, cash reserves, and runway are unknown.

3. Reputation — Severely Damaged

BBB Profile

  • Rating: C+ (NOT accredited)
  • 2 BBB Alerts: (1) Government Actions, (2) Pattern of Complaints
  • Customer review rating: 1.8 out of 5
  • Complaint pages go to at least page 126 (hundreds of formal complaints)
  • BBB has identified a "Pattern of Complaints" the business has failed to resolve

CSLB Probation (Oct 2024)

  • California Contractors State License Board placed Freedom Forever on probation
  • CSLB Case N2022-286, order dated September 4, 2024
  • CSLB Chief White specifically mentioned Freedom Forever when "working with the media to educate consumers about unscrupulous contractors"

Consumer Reviews

  • Consumer Affairs: 390 reviews, heavily negative pattern
  • SolarReviews: "DO NOT USE THIS COMPANY!!!"
  • EcoWatch: "Inconsistent customer service," "concerning pattern of unresponsive or unhelpful support"
  • Reddit r/solar: "Freedom Forever is the shadiest company I've ever dealt with"
  • Facebook: Dedicated complaint group — "Freedom Forever Complaints" (1,000+ members)

Common Complaints

  • Extreme difficulty contacting customer service
  • Installation damage to roofs and property
  • Systems not producing as promised / non-functional systems
  • Contracts/cancellation processing failures — loans activated for uninstalled systems
  • Misleading sales representatives / promises not honored
  • Mechanics liens placed on homeowners' properties (subcontractor non-payment)

4. Subcontractor Payment Problems — CONFIRMED

  • Reddit r/TexasSolar: "Any other contractors not getting paid by Freedom Forever? We're a small roofing company in Texas..."
  • Facebook: "Currently dealing with several unpaid invoices by them over 90 days old. Haven't paid us period actually. Forced to place liens on the..."
  • Consumer Affairs: "If you want your house to have mechanical lien placed on your house then feel free to use them. They did not pay their subcontractors..."
  • CBS Sacramento: Homeowners discovered liens and loans — investigation found Mosaic filed liens against homeowners working with Freedom Forever
  • Freedom Forever FAQ acknowledges lien risk: "Because materials and labor are provided to build your solar project, contractors are allowed to file a mechanics lien..."

Pattern: Subcontractors not paid → subs file mechanics liens on homeowners' properties → homeowners discover unauthorized liens. This is a textbook cash flow distress pattern.

5. Mosaic Bankruptcy Connection — Critical

Timeline

  • June 6, 2025: Mosaic filed Chapter 11 (Southern District of Texas, Case 25-90156)
  • Pre-filing: Mosaic notified all partners of "delays in ALL milestone processing until further notice"
  • ~$130M in unfunded loan commitments frozen at filing
  • September 5, 2025: Court confirmed Mosaic's Plan of Liquidation
  • September 22, 2025: Solar Servicing LLC (Forbright Bank subsidiary) acquired servicing operations

Impact on Freedom Forever

  • Freedom Forever was one of Mosaic's highest-volume installer partners
  • When Mosaic froze milestone payments, it created immediate cash flow crisis for FF
  • Consumers caught between bankrupt lender and installer with no mechanism for resolution
  • Reddit: "Mosaic paid milestone payments to Freedom Forever. Freedom Forever did not complete installation. Mosaic now in bankruptcy cannot process clawback."
  • Unfunded projects were simply closed — installers must find alternative financing
  • Pre-petition amounts owed to installers treated as unsecured claims — "no funds will be available for distribution to unsecured creditors"

6. Financing Partnerships

Freedom Forever works with "four to five of the top six or seven TPO companies" (Solar Builder, Jun 2025). Confirmed partners include GoodLeap and EnFin.

GoodLeap Partnership

Freedom Forever is a confirmed GoodLeap installer partner. GoodLeap's 5,300+ contractor network includes Freedom Forever — meaning GoodLeap's TPO and loan products are being sold through a BBB C+-rated, CSLB-probated installer with confirmed subcontractor payment problems.

EnFin Partnership — Documented Issues

Freedom Forever is a confirmed channel partner for EnFin (Qcells). Joint complaints are severe:

  • Facebook: "We were duped by Freedom Forever and Enfin. They put up our solar panels prior to acquiring ALL required permits..."
  • BBB (EnFin): "Freedom Forever solar installed solar but then... I got a bill for $103,788 from EnFin"
  • Facebook (Yuma AZ): "Trouble with Enfin Freedom Forever solar panels?"
  • Facebook (Sacramento): "Help! Anyone successfully get out of PPA with Enfin and Freedom Forever installers?"

Pattern: Consumers trapped between Freedom Forever (installer) and EnFin (financier), with neither taking responsibility. $100K+ surprise bills, unpermitted installations, inability to exit contracts.

Key Takeaways

  • Freedom Forever is the #2 installer in America but is in full contraction mode — 10 state exits, 20% layoffs, BBB C+, CSLB probation
  • Subcontractor payment problems are confirmed — mechanics liens on homeowners' properties is a textbook cash flow distress pattern
  • Mosaic's Chapter 11 created an immediate financing crisis — FF was a top Mosaic partner and lost access to ~$130M in pipeline
  • Financing partners (GoodLeap, EnFin) absorb reputational damage — joint complaints, $100K surprise bills, unpermitted installs
  • Sunrun is distancing — went from "valued partner" to zero mentions on earnings calls in 12 months
  • Private company with no public financials — actual debt, cash, and runway unknown
  • Displaced dealer network — as FF contracts, their authorized dealers will seek new financing partners. These dealers carry bad sales habits and complaint histories that require rigorous screening.
