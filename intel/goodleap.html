Rating Agency Downgrades — Loan ABS (All Three Agencies)

KBRA (May 2, 2025)

  • 11 downgrades across 18 securitizations collateralized by residential solar + home efficiency loans
  • "Ongoing deterioration in credit performance"
  • Context: Just one year earlier (May 2024), KBRA upgraded 34 classes — the swing to downgrades signals accelerating deterioration
  • Affected trusts: GoodLeap Sustainable Home Solutions (2021-2023), LoanPal Solar Loan (2020-2021), Mill City Solar Loan (2019-2020)
  • All securities have received timely interest payments (for now)

S&P Global (Sep 30, 2025)

  • 13 of 16 ratings downgraded across 6 GoodLeap trusts. Only 3 affirmed.
  • GoodLeap 2022-2, 2022-3, 2022-4, 2023-1: Class A notes dropped to BBB-
  • Class C notes on 2022-2, 2022-3, 2022-4 are undercollateralized
  • Negative excess spread: -1.82% to -3.44%
  • Cumulative default rates: 5.99% to 7.10%, accelerating

Fitch (Jun 6, 2025)

  • Downgraded Class B and C notes across GoodLeap trusts
  • "Prepayments exacerbate negative excess spread"

Fitch (Feb 17, 2026)

  • "Non-Prime Loans and State Concentrations Drive U.S. Solar ABS Loan Defaults"
  • Confirms non-prime borrowers (FICO <700) and state-level concentration (FL, TX, CT) as primary default drivers
  • Validates PLR's 670+ FICO floor and geographic diversification strategy
  • Structural indictment of loan-era underwriting standards that GoodLeap built its business on

KBRA Solar Loan ABS Index (Dec 2025 - Jan 2026)

  • Jan 2026: Early-stage (30-59 day) delinquencies at all-time high. Late-stage (60+ day) at all-time high. Annualized net losses up 173 bps YoY.
  • Dec 2025: ALL major credit metrics hit all-time highs since index inception
  • Partially driven by Sunnova servicer transition (~25% of index) but underlying loan credit trends are genuinely deteriorating
  • This index tracks LOAN ABS only — Palmetto's lease/PPA ABS is NOT in this index

Legal, Regulatory & Reputation Risk

FBI Investigation (Ongoing)

  • Synthetic identity fraud scheme targeting GoodLeap loans in New Orleans area
  • 35+ fraudulent applications approved in New Orleans alone, 20+ in Orleans Parish
  • Scammers used fake SSNs and email addresses paired with real homeowner names/addresses
  • Victims received letters for $45,000+ in loans they never authorized
  • FBI actively investigating; GoodLeap claims <0.05% of transactions are fraudulent

Minnesota AG Lawsuit (Filed March 2024, Ongoing)

  • AG Keith Ellison sued GoodLeap, Sunlight Financial, Solar Mosaic, and Dividend Solar Finance
  • Alleged $35M in deceptive hidden dealer fees across 5,000+ loans
  • GoodLeap specifically: $33M exposure, 853 consumers, average dealer fee 19.32% ($7,552 avg added to balance)
  • Case remanded to Hennepin County District Court (Jan 2025) — still ongoing, no settlement

Multi-State AG Actions (Pink Energy)

  • 17 attorneys general investigating Pink Energy and lending partners including GoodLeap
  • 9 state AGs urged GoodLeap to suspend loan payments for Pink Energy customers

California Class Action

  • Alleged violations of CA consumer protection law
  • GoodLeap arbitration clause prevents class action; forces individual arbitration
  • At least one arbitration went against GoodLeap: $90,000 loan cancelled entirely, $13K awarded to consumer

Debt Commentary & Possible Outcomes

The Core Problem

GoodLeap is a loan company trying to become a TPO company while its legacy loan book burns. Their $8B-$10B cumulative loan ABS issuance is deteriorating across all three rating agencies. The solar loan ABS index is at all-time worst levels. Their TPO program is only 8 months old with 250 contractors and $503M in ABS — tiny compared to PLR's mature platform.

The fundamental question: Can Wall Street trust your TPO paper when your loan paper is toxic? GoodLeap's ABS investors are watching loan delinquencies hit record highs, rating agencies downgrade en masse, and an FBI investigation unfold. That creates a narrative credibility problem that no amount of TPO issuance can quickly fix.

Scenario 1: Managed TPO Transition (40%)

GoodLeap successfully pivots from loans to TPO, growing TIP Solar deals while running off the legacy loan book. The $1.5B Apollo/TIP partnership provides capacity. They gradually expand from 15 to 25+ states and grow contractor count. Loan ABS degradation stabilizes as older vintages age out.

Scenario 2: Legacy Loan Book Drags Them Down (35%)

Loan ABS delinquencies continue accelerating. More rating downgrades. ABS investors lose confidence across BOTH loan and TPO tracks. Legal exposure compounds (MN AG settlement, FBI investigation resolution, more state AGs). The $17.7B valuation collapses. IPO window closes permanently.

  • opportunity: GoodLeap's brand toxicity makes the safe choice for EPCs and ABS investors
  • opportunity: Shared EPCs shift wallet share to as GoodLeap tightens in FL/TX/VA

Scenario 3: Acquisition / Strategic Partnership (20%)

Apollo/ATLAS SP already structured the TIP Solar deals. A larger player (Apollo itself, Blackstone, a utility) could acquire GoodLeap's TPO platform while letting the loan book run off in SPVs. The $17.7B valuation makes outright acquisition expensive, but a distressed sale of the TPO platform alone could happen.

Scenario 4: Slow Decline (5%)

Both loan and TPO businesses stagnate. Legal costs mount. Employee attrition accelerates (Glassdoor already declining). No IPO. No acquisition. GoodLeap becomes a zombie — still operating but losing relevance.

  • opportunity: Effortless market share gains as GoodLeap fades
