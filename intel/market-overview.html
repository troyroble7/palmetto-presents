Market Context (Updated Feb 28, 2026)

Core 2026 Forecast (SEIA/Wood Mac/Ohm/EnergySage)

  • 2025 full year: ~4.3-4.7 GW. Q4-25 revised up on extended 25D demand pull-forward
  • 2026 = trough year: 3.5 GW (-18-26% YoY). Market bottoms then recovers. Total US new capacity: 86 GW record (SEIA).
  • TPO share: 69-70% in 2026 (up from 45% in 2025) — SEIA/Wood Mac/Ohm all confirm. TPO is now the ONLY way homeowners access a federal tax credit. Barclays: could reach 90%+.
  • EnergySage H2 2025 (Feb 27, 2026): 205% YoY increase in homeowner engagement from ITC urgency. Most installers hit annual capacity by Oct 2025. Modules sold out through year-end 2026 — supply constraints favor established TPO providers.
  • Residential pricing: $2.49/W median H2 2025 (+0.4%). Storage: $1,074/kWh (+3.6%).
  • Storage attachment: 38% national Q3-25, CA 79%, CA NEM3 87%, HI 92%. Sunrun at 71%. 1,026 MWh deployed Q3-25 (+40% YoY).
  • Median solar APR: 7.99% in Oct-25 (up from 4.49% in early 2025). Fed cuts NOT translating to lower solar rates.
  • Utility rates: +7.4% YoY nationally. Data center demand tripling electricity needs by 2030. FPL +$945M (Jan '26), +$705M (2027). APS requesting 14% mid-2026.

Policy (as of Feb 28, 2026)

  • OBBBA (signed Jul 4, 2025): 25D (homeowner ITC) EXPIRED Dec 31, 2025. 48E (TPO/commercial ITC) active through Dec 31, 2027 with safe harbor to 2030. FEOC MACR: 40% (2026) → 60% (2030+).
  • NPR national coverage (Feb 20, 2026): "Solar installers switched to leases after tax credits ended" — features model as 25D→48E shift exemplar.

Tariffs (Updated Feb 28, 2026)

  • Section 201 tariffs EXPIRED Feb 6, 2026 — first time in 8 years without
  • NEW (Feb 25, 2026): US Commerce Dept: 126% tariffs on Indian solar panels, 86-143% Indonesia, 81% Laos. Manufacturers relocated from SE Asia (already tariffed) to India/Indonesia/Laos — now tariffed too.
  • Definitive decision: July 6, 2026
  • Section 232 investigation on polysilicon initiated July 2025 — could impact all solar products
  • Chinese LFP batteries: ~82% combined import duties (Section 301 + reciprocal)
  • Impact: Module shortages + tariffs = supply constraints. Established TPO providers (PLR) with locked supply chains benefit. QCells (EnFin parent, domestic mfg) is named petitioner on tariff case.
  • CFPB PACE rule effective March 1, 2026 — ability-to-repay assessments for PACE loans

  • Top 10 resi states (Q3-25 MW): CA 297, PR 114, FL 82, TX 77, IL 52, MA 50, NY 49, AZ 41, NJ 34, CT 32
  • 2026-2030 CAGR: 7% (Ohm). Resi forecast to more than triple by 2035 (96+ GWdc, Wood Mac).

Pre-Paid Lease (PPL) Category — Consolidated Intel

  • Ohm Analytics forecast: 230 MW PPL volume in 2026, ~10% of market by Q4-26
  • 4 PPL competitors analyzed: Propel/Concert, HDM Capital, Participate Energy, Thrive Power
  • All share same IRS risk: 48E commercial treatment of residential assets is untested at scale
  • FEOC squeeze (WoodMac): FEOC MACR starts at 40% in 2026, rising to 60% by 2030. PPL players with restrictive AVLs (Propel = QCells-only, Participate = Tesla-only) face supply chain bifurcation risk. ~50% of US module capacity could be FEOC-impacted.
  • Backend credit union financing: Most PPL customers do NOT pay cash — they finance the prepayment through a high-interest credit union loan at 8-10%+ APR. PPL's "$0 monthly" pitch is misleading — homeowners have monthly payments to a lender, just not to the solar company. Only true cash buyers avoid monthly costs. When credit union interest is added, PPL's total cost advantage over shrinks or disappears.
  • Consolidated PPL memory file: See ppl-competitive-intel.md in memory/ — pricing caps, credit, geography, AVL, ownership timelines, weaknesses for all 4
