Pre-Paid Lease (PPL) Competitive Intelligence — Consolidated

Last updated: Mar 3, 2026

PPL Category Overview

  • Emerging financing model: Homeowner pays upfront (60-78% depending on provider ITC pass-through), company owns system for 5-6 years, claims 48E commercial ITC + domestic content + energy community bonuses + MACRS depreciation, then transfers ownership
  • Ohm Analytics forecast: 230 MW PPL volume in 2026, ~10% of market by Q4-26
  • Post-ITC tailwind: 25D residential ITC expired — PPL is the primary mechanism for homeowners to still access ITC savings through third-party ownership
  • All 4 PPL players share the same fundamental IRS risk: 48E commercial treatment of residential assets is untested at scale

OBBBA / 48E Cliff — Existential Timeline (WoodMac/SEIA Q3 2025)

  • 48E/45Y expire after Dec 31, 2027 — ALL PPL players lose their entire economic model
  • safe harbor advantage: TPO providers can use 5% Safe Harbor OR Physical Work Test for projects <1.5 MW through 2030 per Aug 15, 2025 Treasury guidance
  • FEOC MACR schedule: 40% (2026) → 45% (2027) → 50% (2028) → 55% (2029) → 60% (2030+). PPL players with restrictive AVLs face supply chain risk — ~50% of US module capacity could be FEOC-impacted.

Market Context (Ohm Q3-25 + WoodMac/SEIA Q3 2025)

  • 2026 = trough year: Ohm: 3.7 GW (-20% YoY, revised up from 3.5 GW/-25%). WoodMac: 4,185 MW (-13%). EPCs desperate for volume.
  • TPO share (Ohm Q3-25): 42% nationally, 53% in key states, forecast ~64% in 2026. Cash/loan collapsing from ~2.7 GW to 1.1 GW (-59% YoY).
  • PPL forecast (Ohm): 230 MW in 2026, ~10% of market by Q4-26. Contractor survey: 44% of TPO expected to be PPL. Faster adoption than expected. Enphase entering PPL market via large distributor partnership.
  • Storage attachment (Ohm Q3-25): 38% national, HI 92%, CA NEM3 87%, CA 79%, IL 48%, AZ 46%. No PPL player offers battery-only — major gap.
  • Residential pricing (Ohm Q3-25): $3.59/W median (-10% YoY). PPL caps ($4.50-$4.85/W) are 25-35% above market. Contractors expect TPO +6% in 2026.
  • FL opportunity: Only 5% penetration, 178 GW remaining TAM. GoodLeap retreating. PPL players barely present in FL (only Thrive "claims" it).
  • PPL scaling concerns (Ohm): "growing, but too soon to determine which will scale." Capital constraints + untested IRS treatment + 48E 2027 cliff = systemic risk.

PPL ITC Pass-Through & Prepayment Ratios (Feb 2026)

How it works: PPL company owns the system, claims the 48E commercial ITC, and assigns a portion of that ITC value as a discount to the homeowner. The homeowner's prepayment = system price minus ITC discount. Homeowner either pays prepayment in cash or finances it through a credit union loan. CU loan financing is standard across ALL PPL products — not provider-specific.

PPL ProviderITC Value AssignedHO Prepay %MultiplierSource

Key insight: Propel at 60% prepay is the MOST competitive PPL (lowest homeowner cost). If beats Propel on monthly, it beats all PPL providers.

For CU loan modeling: Multiply system price by prepay % above, then amortize via CU loan. Known CU terms: Propel/Concert 25-yr @ 8.79%, Participate 20-yr @ 8.49% (6-mo deferred), HDM CU terms TBD.

Example (8 kW + 13.5 kWh battery, $52,250 system):

  • Propel: $31,350 prepay → $259/mo CU → ($165/mo) saves $94/mo
  • Participate: $36,575 → $307/mo → saves $142/mo
  • Thrive: $39,188 → $329/mo → saves $164/mo
  • HDM: $40,755 → $342/mo → saves $177/mo

1. Propel / Concert Finance / Enphase

Full analysis: ~/Documents/Brain/Propel (Concert-Enphase) vs Palmetto — Competitive Analysis.md

Memory file: propel-concert-enphase-intel.md

Source (Q1 2026): ~/Downloads/Propel Sales Training_Q1 2026.pptx — official beta launch training deck

Product Structure (from Q1 2026 Sales Training)

  • Official name: Prepaid Energy Services Agreement (ESA) paired with Concert loan = "Propel"
  • Model: Transitional ownership — homeowner finances prepaid ESA via Concert loan, exercises Early Buyout Option (EBO) at year 5 to take ownership
  • EBO options: Year 5, 6.5, or 8 (recommended year 5 for maximum benefit)
  • ESA & Loan must transfer together on home sale (unless loan already paid off)
  • Design tool: Solargraf (proposals, pricing, financing selection)
  • Fulfillment: Greentech Renewables Design Services handles final designs, plan sets, and BOM
  • Contract execution: Concert's "Switch" platform (ESA + Loan Note in one DocuSign packet)
  • Credit check: Soft inquiry (QuickCheck) in Switch — hardens after loan pays for equipment
  • DTA: Digital Terms of Acceptance + Automatic Payments Election sent post-signing

Pricing (Updated Q1 2026 — from Sales Training Deck)

  • Max PV $/W by state: $3.60/W (AZ), $3.70/W (TX), $4.00/W (ME) — LOWER than previously reported $4.85/W
  • Max storage: $2,000/kWh | Loan range: $10,001-$135,000
  • Min productivity: 1,000 kWh/kW (AZ & TX), 900 kWh/kW (ME)
  • Energy community pricing: 40% discount in SD, 41% in TX (10% EC ITC adder)
  • Outside energy communities: discount drops significantly — model heavily dependent on EC zones

3. Participate Energy

Full analysis: ~/Documents/Brain/Participate Energy vs Palmetto — Competitive Analysis.md

Two Products

  • CCA On-Bill Tariff (CA only): $0 upfront, NO credit check, NO lien, payment on utility bill, works for RENTERS. Tesla-only equipment. 10+ CCA partners serving 2M+ Californians.
  • Prepaid Lease (9 states): Customer pays ~70% upfront, ownership at FMV after year 6. AZ, CA, CO, HI, ID, NJ, NY, TX, UT.

EPC Payment Terms

  • 70/30 split: 70% paid at install, 30% paid at PTO

Homeowner Payment Options (70% Prepay)

  • Option A: Cash upfront — homeowner pays 70% out of pocket
  • Option B: CU loan — homeowner finances 70% via credit union loan (same model as Propel/Concert)
  • CU loan terms: 20-year term, 8.49% APR, 6 months deferred payments
  • 30% ITC discount applied upfront (Participate retains remainder)
  • CU loan is worse than Propel/Concert on term (20 yr vs 25 yr = higher monthly) despite slightly lower APR (8.49% vs 8.79%)

Pricing

  • CCA: $0 upfront, on-bill tariff (utility bill payment)
  • Prepaid Lease: ~70% of system cost upfront (financed via CU loan)
  • CARE/FERA: Up to 60% bill reduction (income-qualified, SGIP-funded)
  • No disclosed pricing caps

Key Weaknesses

  • CCA model only works in California
  • Tesla-only equipment limits installer flexibility
  • ~14 employees — severe scaling constraints
  • No ABS, no securitization, no disclosed funding
  • Prepaid Lease requires ~70% upfront capital
  • Only 9 states for prepaid lease
  • CU loan pairing = higher monthly than PLR: 20-year term / 8.49% APR → higher monthly payment than PLR's 25-year lease despite lower upfront. 6-month deferral = payment shock risk.
  • 70/30 EPC terms entice installers with faster cash flow but PLR's milestone funding is more structured

Where Participate Wins vs - No UCC lien on property

  • Works for RENTERS (CCA model) — market cannot serve
  • On-bill tariff = 98%+ payment rates, zero collections
  • Government trust channel (CCA endorsement)
  • SD overlap: Baker Home Energy dual-channeling with CEA Solar Plus

San Diego Connection

  • Clean Energy Alliance (CEA) serves 250K+ customers in North County SD
  • Direct competitive collision with PLR's SD anchors

4. Thrive Power LLC

Full analysis: ~/Documents/Brain/Thrive Power vs Palmetto — Competitive Analysis.md

Pricing

  • Pre-Paid PPA: ~$2.60/W including 13.5 kWh battery (per Reddit)
  • $0 monthly payments, no escalators
  • Thrive retains ownership, claims ITC, passes savings as upfront discount
  • Possible ownership transfer at ~year 6

Key Weaknesses

  • ~2-3 year old company with ZERO track record
  • No named leadership — complete organizational opacity
  • No institutional capital, ABS, or disclosed funding
  • TriSMART Solar SHUT DOWN end of 2025 — confirmed by Reuters + Houston Press. Customers left with incomplete installations. PRIMARY installer partner is DEAD.
  • Nov 2025 prepayment verification formalization = dealer fund-handling problems
  • THREAT LEVEL DOWNGRADED TO LOW — TriSMART shutdown removes operational backbone
  • Brand confusion — 5+ "Thrive" solar entities pollute search results
  • Password-protected product pages (still in development)
  • No VPP program despite installing batteries
  • "All 50 states" claim unverified

Where Thrive Wins vs - Pre-Paid PPA at $2.60/W with battery is genuinely aggressive pricing

  • Zero monthly payments, zero escalators
  • Battery INCLUDED in base price (not add-on)
  • Zero collections risk (prepaid model)
  • Serves ITC-ineligible homeowners (retirees, low tax liability)

Critical Insight

  • The company is weak. The model is strong.
  • If a well-capitalized competitor adopts the prepaid PPA, it becomes a real threat
  • Watch for capital events (tax equity partner, PE backing)
