US Residential Solar Market Outlook 2026

Market Size & Forecasts

  • Q3 2025 resi (Ohm): 1,154 MW (-8% YoY), +6% QoQ — 25D pull-forward stronger than expected
  • 2025 full year (Ohm): 4.7 GW (-8% YoY). Q4-25 revised up to +4% YoY on extended 25D demand
  • 2026 = trough year (Ohm): 3.7 GW (-20% YoY, up from prior 3.5 GW/-25%). WoodMac: 4,185 MW (-13%)
  • 2026-2030 CAGR (Ohm): 7%. Market bottoms 2026, then sustained recovery driven by utility rates, electrification, TPO adoption
  • SEIA: Resi solar grows 3% avg annually 2025-2030 (after 2026 dip)
  • WoodMac: Downgraded resi near-term by 2% (2025) and 8% (2026)
  • Total US solar (all segments): 246 GWdc base case 2025-2030 (4% lower than pre-OBBBA)
  • BNEF: US may deploy 53 GW in 2025, 61 GW in 2026 (all segments)
  • McKinsey: "Down, not out" -- reset, not collapse
  • Top 10 resi states Q3-25 (Ohm MW): CA 297, PR 114, FL 82, TX 77, IL 52, MA 50, NY 49, AZ 41, NJ 34, CT 32

Pricing

  • Median resi price (Ohm Q3-25): $3.59/W (-10% YoY)
  • Median solar APR (Ohm Oct-25): 7.99% (up from 4.49% in early 2025). Fed cuts NOT translating to lower solar rates
  • Contractors expect: TPO pricing +6% in 2026 (Ohm survey)
  • Module wholesale: as low as $0.49/W
  • Without 25D: cash/loan purchases lose ~$20K in 25-year savings
  • GoodLeap dealer fees averaging $7,552/loan
  • Enphase ADLP price increase: 10-20% on select microinverters effective Feb 9, 2026 — upward pressure on equipment costs
  • Fed funds: 3.5-3.75% | 10-yr T-Bill: 4.0%

TPO Dominance (Ohm Q3-25)

  • TPO share: 42% nationally Q3-25, 53% in key states; forecast ~64% in 2026
  • Could reach 65-75% by end of 2026
  • 25D expired -> 48E (TPO) is ONLY path to ITC for homeowners
  • Cash/loan market collapsing: Contracting from ~2.7 GW to 1.1 GW (-59% YoY)
  • PPL forecast: 230 MW in 2026, ~10% of market by Q4-26. Contractor survey: 44% of TPO expected PPL
  • Loan-purchased systems no longer qualify for federal tax credit

Storage Attach Rates (Ohm Q3-25)

  • National: 38% Q3-25 (+40% YoY). 1,026 MWh deployed Q3-25. 2025 projected: 4,013 MWh (+49%)
  • State rates: HI 92%, CA NEM3 87%, CA 79%, IL 48%, NC 48%, AZ 46%, TX 31%, FL 15%
  • 2026 forecast: 3.9 GW residential storage
  • Sunrun: 18% (Q2-23) -> 62% (Q4-24) -> 70% (Q3-25)
  • Q4 2025 expected record quarter for resi battery installs

Net Metering Changes

  • 386 state/utility policy changes proposed/pending/decided in 2025
  • CA NEM 3.0: -75% export rates, demand fell ~80%, Supreme Court ordered reconsideration (Aug 2025)
  • CA = 48% of national resi decline in 2024
  • NJ SREC values dropping $85 -> $76.50 on Mar 13, 2026
  • Trend: states shifting to "value of DG" and advanced rate designs

Supply Chain

  • US domestic module capacity surpassed 60 GW (Oct 2025, +37% from Dec 2024)
  • 65 new/expanded manufacturing facilities came online in 2025
  • Every major solar supply chain component now made in US
  • FEOC 2026: 50% non-FEOC content rising to 85% by 2030
  • Risk: 30% deployment drop if FEOC enforced without sufficient domestic supply
  • New bottlenecks: transformers and switchgear

Labor Market

  • 370,556 solar workers in US (IREC Census)
  • 368,000 additional workers needed by 2034
  • 40,000+ electricians needed beyond current projections
  • Limited EPC/labor availability = major impediment (SEIA Q4 2025)
  • Solar installer = one of fastest-growing jobs

Regional Trends (Ohm Q3-25)

  • Growing: FL 82 MW (+11% YoY), TX 77 MW (+6%), IL 52 MW (+16%), AZ 41 MW (+38%), CT 32 MW (+33%)
  • Shrinking: CA 297 MW (-17% YoY), PR 114 MW (-5%), NJ 34 MW (-26%)
  • Utility rates: +7.4% YoY nationally (EIA through Sep-25). Data center demand tripling electricity needs by 2030 — bullish for solar
  • Diversification: 41 states + PR each installed 100+ MW in 2024
  • TX, CA, FL = 43% of capacity

Outlook Scenarios

  • Bear (20%): Resi declines 10-15%, FEOC shortages, more BKs
  • Base (60%): Resi declines 2-8%, TPO dominant 65-70%, storage >50%, stabilizes H2 2026
  • Bull (20%): TPO smooth, rate cuts, CA NEM 3.0 relief, resi flat to slightly positive

VPP Growth (Ohm Q3-25)

  • 35 states + DC advanced VPP/DER aggregation policies in 2025
  • Ohm Analytics: VPP enrollments +222% in 2025, 21% overall VPP capacity growth
  • Key programs: Sunrun VPP (106K enrolled), ConnectedSolutions (MA/CT/RI), LUMA CBES+ (PR, 498.5 MW)
  • US energy storage Q2 2025: 5.6 GW installed (new quarterly record)
  • US expected to double utility-scale battery storage to ~65 GW by end 2026

Supply Chain (Ohm Q3-25)

  • Modules: Qcells ~35% share, LONGi ~10%, Silfab ~8%, REC ~7%, Canadian Solar ~6%
  • Inverters: Enphase 37% (#1), Tesla 29% (#2, via PW3), SolarEdge 22% (#3)
  • Storage OEMs: Tesla ~30-47%, Enphase ~25%, SolarEdge ~15%, FranklinWH mid-single digits, Lunar <1%
  • Enphase ADLP: 10-20% price increase on select microinverters effective Feb 9, 2026
  • Qcells UFLPA detention + DomCon supply constraints = upward pressure on equipment costs H1-2026
  • Installer rankings (Q3-25): Sunrun 15.9% (-7% YoY), Freedom Forever 6.4%, Tesla 3.2%, Trinity 2.2%
