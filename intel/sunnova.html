Sunnova Bankruptcy Intelligence (Updated Feb 2026)

Detailed Timeline

  • Feb 2025: Cut 15% of workforce for "capital efficiency"
  • Mar 2025: Warned investors could run out of cash
  • May 28, 2025: Trump DOE revoked $2.92B loan guarantee (death blow)
  • Jun 1, 2025: Subsidiary TEP Developer filed Ch.11
  • Jun 5, 2025: 55% workforce layoff (~718 employees)
  • Jun 9, 2025: Sunnova Energy International Ch.11 filed ($10.67B debt, $13.5M cash). Case No. 25-90160, S.D. Texas.
  • Jul 31, 2025: Sale approved to Solaris Assets LLC (GoodFinch Management). Credit bid $90M DIP + $25M cash + cure costs.
  • Sep 4, 2025: Solaris completed acquisition
  • Nov 12, 2025: Plan confirmed. Nov 14 effective date. Total case: ~155 days.
  • Sunnova.com now displays "Sunnova Closure and Support Resources"

SunStrong = New Entity

  • Manages 500K+ solar customers, 4 GW distributed generation + storage
  • Also acquired legacy SunPower assets (2024 bankruptcy)
  • Former Sunnova CEO Paul Mathews = CRO at SunStrong
  • Working with GoodLeap to complete in-progress installations
  • ATLAS negotiating directly with certain dealers for in-process systems

Dealer Network Status

  • Pre-BK: ~450 dealers; at filing: ~175 active
  • ~275 dealers left pre-bankruptcy (couldn't get paid)
  • Dispersed to: Sunrun, PLR, GoodLeap, EnFin, Freedom Forever
  • TEPH paid $15M for certain systems + dealer settlement rights

DOE Loan Disposition

  • $371.6M in already-issued DOE-guaranteed bonds (Project Hestia) NOT included in restructured debt
  • Remaining ~$2.9B formally "de-obligated" back to federal government
  • Government unlikely to lose money

Root Causes of Collapse

  • $10.67B debt vs $13.5M cash
  • Rising interest rates compressed margins
  • California NEM 3.0 killed largest market (CA installs fell 45%)
  • Trump DOE revoked $2.92B loan guarantee (May 28, 2025)
  • Resi solar installs fell 31% in 2024 (six consecutive YoY decline quarters)
  • Couldn't pay dealers in early 2025 -> origination halted

Key Insight

Sunnova's underlying solar assets performed well -- portfolio "stable, paying interest" during BK. The failure was overleveraged capital structure + policy shocks, NOT the TPO model itself.

TPO Market Paradox

TPO companies fail, but TPO model thrives:

  • Current share (Ohm Q3-25): 42% nationally, 53% in key states
  • 2026 forecast: 64% (Ohm), potentially 75%+ by end of 2026
  • Cash/loan contracting from ~2.7 GW to 1.1 GW (-59% YoY)
  • PPL emerging: 230 MW forecast 2026, 44% of TPO per contractor survey
  • 25D eliminated -> 48E (TPO) is ONLY way homeowners access ITC economics
