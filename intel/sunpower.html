SunPower / Complete Solar Intelligence (Feb 2026)

Company Overview

  • CEO: T.J. Rodgers (former Cypress Semiconductor CEO)
  • Ticker: SPWR (NASDAQ), ~$1.73/share (analyst target $5.81)
  • Market Position: #5 US residential solar installer (Ohm Analytics)
  • Revenue: FY2025 $308.8M, Q4'25 record $88.5M, targeting $1B by 2028
  • Profitability: 4 consecutive profitable quarters in FY25, $10.9M operating income
  • Salesforce: 2,027+ reps across 45 states
  • Cash: $9.3M (thin for $300M+ company), $105M in equity facilities

Origin Story

  • Aug 2024: Original SunPower filed Ch.11 (four consecutive years of operating losses)
  • Sep 30, 2024: Complete Solaria acquired Blue Raven, New Homes Division, Non-Installing Dealer Network, and "SunPower" brand for $45M
  • Apr 21, 2025: Rebranded as SunPower (SPWR), new "Helios" logo

REC Technology Partnership

  • Joint development agreement (Jan 7, 2026) with REC Group (Singapore)
  • Current: "Monolith" panel -- REC Alpha Pure-TX 470W (SunPower exclusive US rights)
  • Future: Bifacial upgrade to 500+ watts
  • Returns SunPower to technology-differentiation roots

ITC Impact Assessment (T.J. Rodgers)

  • Resi solar pricing: $3.30/W -> $3.88/W (+17.6%) without 25D
  • SunPower volume loss: ~134 MW; revenue ~$80M -> ~$74.2M/qtr (-7.2%)
  • Rodgers argues subsidies harm solar by imposing regulations + fostering dependency
  • Ambia acquisition timed to build scale ahead of ITC phase-out

Risks to Monitor

  • Integration risk: 3 acquisitions in 6 months, OpEx up 41% sequentially
  • Financial fragility: $9.3M cash, $105M equity facilities = dilution risk
  • Salesforce quality: 841 to 2,027 reps in 7 months -- watch NPS/collections
  • Stock at $1.73: Market skeptical. Equity-funded growth gets harder if stock drops
  • Partner vs competitor tension: As SunPower scales, may seek to internalize TPO financing
