New Homes Customer Complaints — Overpricing

Pricing Example (Taylor Morrison / Sunrun, CA): Buyer received Sunrun quote of $32,000 to purchase or $110/month for 25 years. Same system quoted at ~$11,500 from Tesla after tax credits. That is a 2.8x markup. When challenged, Sunrun responded: "The builder has an exclusive agreement with us — either you pay Sunrun or you don't buy the home."
  • Escalator clauses: New construction leases include 0%-3.5% annual escalators. A 2.99% escalator turns $110/month into $400-450/month over 25 years. Multiple buyers report not understanding this at closing.
  • Forced contracts: Multiple reports of new homebuyers discovering solar leases attached to their purchase that they didn't fully understand. In some cases, listing agents failed to disclose the lease existed.
  • Lease transfer nightmares: Sellers face 680+ FICO credit checks on buyers, refusal to transfer to LLCs, 30-60 day processing delays that blow up closings, and buyout costs of $20,000-$52,000 to exit.

New Homes — Strategic Concerns

  • Earnings silence: Zero mention of new homes in Q4 2025 earnings call — suggests the segment is not performing as expected or is de-prioritized
  • Less than 5% of volume: Despite hiring two SunPower VPs and projecting 50%+ growth, new homes remains immaterial to Sunrun's financials
  • Builder captive model: Exclusive builder deals force homebuyers into Sunrun at inflated pricing — creating brand resentment and regulatory risk
  • Sunnova bankruptcy fallout: Lennar/SunStreet is available, but Sunrun's financial position (30%+ stock crash, affiliate cuts) may not inspire builder confidence
  • Base Power threat: $4B-valuation battery-as-a-service startup with exclusive Lennar partnership in TX. National expansion planned. Purpose-built for new construction.

Debt Commentary & Possible Outcomes

Scenario 1: Managed Deleveraging (Base Case — 50%)

Sunrun has $823M cash, no recourse maturities until March 2028, and is generating $350-450M/yr cash. They can service the debt and slowly pay down recourse obligations. The $14.7B non-recourse debt is asset-backed and ring-fenced. This is what management is executing: defensive posture, affiliate cuts, margin over volume. They survive but shrink.

Scenario 2: Strategic Asset Sales / JV Acceleration (30%)

The Hannon Armstrong JV ($500M) and the shift to 50% asset-sale structures in Q4 signal Sunrun may increasingly sell off system portfolios. This reduces their long-term recurring revenue moat — the VPP/grid services story depends on owning assets.

Scenario 3: Distress / Restructuring (15%)

$1B/year interest on a company guiding $350M cash generation is razor-thin. If interest rates stay elevated, ABS markets tighten, or volume drops further, the math breaks. Altman Z-Score of -0.47 and 46.5% bankruptcy probability are not theoretical.

Scenario 4: Acquisition Target (5%)

At $2.9B market cap with $22.6B in assets, Sunrun trades at ~13 cents on the dollar. A utility, infrastructure fund, or energy company could acquire for the fleet (1M subscribers, 4 GWh storage, 18 VPP programs). Likely acquirers: NextEra, AES, Brookfield, Blackstone.

Business Model Shift — Asset Sales

  • Q4 2025: 50% of all systems sold to third parties (up from 10% in Q3)
  • Sunrun retains O&M rights, grid services revenue, and cross-sell capability
  • The $692M in "Energy systems & product sales" revenue (up 433% YoY) is almost entirely from these asset sales — it flatters the top line but isn't recurring
  • This undermines the "own the fleet" narrative that justified the VPP/grid services premium

Affiliate Channel Collapse

  • 40%+ reduction in affiliate volume guided for 2026
  • Sunrun is cutting lower-quality EPCs and concentrating with direct installs
  • Hundreds of EPCs will lose their Sunrun TPO option in 2026
