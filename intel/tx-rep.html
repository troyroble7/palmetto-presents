Key 2026 Trends

  • Death of 1:1 net metering — virtually no TX REP offers dollar-for-dollar credit anymore
  • Fixed buyback rates declining — landing at 3-5 cents/kWh for most REPs
  • Real-time/wholesale plans rising — allow higher earnings during summer peaks but carry price risk
  • VPP programs emerging as the new value driver — $40/mo (GoodLeap/Reliant), $10/mo (Tesla), $20/mo (SOLRITE) for battery participation
  • TX does NOT mandate net metering — each REP decides independently whether to offer buyback
  • 85-90% of new TX solar installs include battery storage
  • 25D ITC expired Jan 1, 2026 — massive shift to TPO and prepaid models
  • ERCOT DRRS launching 2026 — will register residential batteries/solar, potentially enabling future grid services revenue

Key VPP Takeaways

  • Palmetto Battery Rewards ($4/kWh/mo) delivers the highest per-kWh credit in the market. Example: 13.5 kWh battery = $54/mo; 27 kWh = $108/mo.
  • GoodLeap/Reliant ($40/mo flat) is simpler but lower value for larger batteries. Announced Nov 24, 2025. Reliant (NRG subsidiary) + GoodLeap GoodGrid partnership. 1.5 GW target over 5 years.
  • SOLRITE/sonnen ($20/mo, 12¢/kWh) is the only battery-ONLY VPP (no solar required). Launched Feb 11, 2026. First battery-only VPP in Texas. Targeting "solar orphans." 3,000 customers, targeting 10,000 by EOY 2026. TX deregulated only (Houston, Corpus Christi, McAllen, DFW).
  • Tesla Dynamic Plan requires full battery purchase (no leasing). $10/mo VPP credit per Powerwall. Cybertruck V2G program launched Feb 11, 2026 in CenterPoint/Oncor territories.
  • Base Power is the emerging threat — $4B valuation, $1.3B funding, Lennar partnership in Austin/DFW new construction. El Paso Electric 10 MW pilot (Feb 2026) marks first move into regulated utilities. ~250 MWh deployed statewide.

PPL vs Palmetto in TX

  • Palmetto advantage: $0 upfront, 1:1 buyback, $4/kWh battery credit, multi-vendor hardware, 25-year O&M, ABS-backed capital
  • PPL advantage: No monthly solar payment after prepayment (if paid cash), ownership transfer at year 5-6
  • Reality check: Most PPL customers finance via credit union loan at 8-10%+ APR — they still have monthly payments, just to a different lender. Total cost often equals or exceeds Palmetto's all-in lease.
  • 48E cliff: ALL PPL players lose economics after Dec 31, 2027. Palmetto TPO safe harbored through 2030.

Strategic Summary

Palmetto TX Position — Strengths

  • Highest per-kWh battery credit in market ($4/kWh/mo vs flat $40/mo or 12¢/kWh competitors)
  • 1:1 solar buyback while market moves to 3-5¢/kWh fixed rates
  • $0 upfront vs PPL $12-30K prepayments
  • Multi-vendor hardware (Tesla PW3, Enphase, Franklin) vs single-brand competitors
  • 25-year full O&M including battery replacement
  • Safe harbored through 2030 via 48E — 4 years of runway vs PPL's 2 years
  • Pre-activation discount (50% on flagship plans) incentivizes early enrollment

Key Competitive Threats in TX

  • Base Power ($4B valuation): Battery-as-a-service, Lennar new homes partnership, expanding into regulated utilities. Primary threat to new construction market.
  • GoodLeap/Reliant GoodGrid ($40/mo): Flat-rate VPP simplicity may appeal to customers who prefer predictability over per-kWh optimization.
  • HDM in TX: Prepaid PPA gaining traction with EPCs looking for cash-buyer economics.
  • Propel beta in TX: $3.70/W cap compresses EPC margins — but compelling in energy community zones with 40% discount.

Market Opportunity

  • TX is 2nd in US solar capacity, added 7.4 GW in first 9 months of 2025
  • 85-90% battery co-installation rate on new installs
  • 25D expiration driving massive TPO shift — Palmetto's sweet spot
  • ERCOT DRRS could create new revenue streams for VPP participants
  • Chariot Energy dominates solar customer satisfaction — but doesn't offer battery credits or TPO financing
