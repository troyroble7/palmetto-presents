The Future of Solar Finance

What is Solar 3.0?

The solar industry has evolved through three distinct eras. Solar 3.0 represents the shift from customer acquisition to long-term customer value creation.

The Three Eras of Residential Solar

To understand where the industry is going, you need to understand where it's been.

1.0
The Pioneers
2006 – 2015

Cash sales and early loans. Solar was expensive, niche, and driven by environmental early adopters. Financing was limited.

2.0
The Boom
2016 – 2024

TPO and loan products exploded. Customer acquisition at all costs. Volume mattered more than quality. Many finance providers cut corners.

3.0
Long-Term Value
2025 – Forward

The best finance providers are enterprise partners, not just capital providers. Quality, customer experience, and 25-year asset health define the winners.

Why Solar 2.0 Broke

Solar 2.0 was defined by a simple equation: acquire customers as fast as possible, securitize the contracts, repeat. The problem? Nobody was thinking about what happened after the sale.

  • Sunnova filed Chapter 11 in June 2025 — customers report months-long repair delays
  • Mosaic filed Chapter 11 in June 2025 — ~$130M in unfunded projects stranded consumers
  • GoodLeap's loan ABS is being downgraded by all three rating agencies — delinquencies at all-time highs
  • Sunrun's Net Subscriber Value collapsed 30% in a single year — stock crashed 35%

The root cause is the same across all of them: poor system controls led to consumers who felt misled, who then stopped paying, which degraded ABS performance. Solar 2.0 treated customers as transactions. Solar 3.0 treats them as 25-year relationships.

What Solar 3.0 Means

Solar 3.0 is built on a simple idea: the real value isn't in acquiring a customer — it's in serving them over 25 years.

Solar 3.0 = Long-term customer value creation, not just customer acquisition.

Cross-sell storage, generators, water heaters, and HVAC across the 25-year contract life. The finance provider becomes an enterprise partner, not just a capital provider.

The Four Pillars of Solar 3.0

  • Quality Over Volume: EPC partners are held to operational excellence standards. Tiers are earned through 6 KPIs — install quality, backlog management, NPS, collections, tranche execution, and volume — not bought with deal flow.
  • 25-Year Customer Relationship: Every homeowner who signs a lease is a customer for 25 years. That means cross-sell opportunities for battery backup, generators, water heaters, and HVAC — creating recurring revenue streams for EPC partners.
  • ABS Portfolio Health: The finance provider's cost of capital is directly tied to customer satisfaction. Happy customers pay on time. On-time payments produce clean ABS. Clean ABS gets the tightest spreads. Tightest spreads fund the best pricing for EPCs and homeowners.
  • Consumer Protection: Solar 3.0 providers reject bad actors. When EPCs with BBB complaints, subcontractor non-payment, or regulatory actions are denied access to the platform, the entire ecosystem benefits.

Why It Matters for EPC Partners

For EPCs, Solar 3.0 means a fundamental shift in how you think about your finance partners:

  • Revenue per customer increases — cross-sell products mean more projects per rooftop over 25 years
  • Lower cost of capital = better pricing — Solar 3.0 providers with clean ABS get tighter spreads, which fund more competitive homeowner pricing
  • Lead generation — media platforms and brand campaigns drive inbound leads directly to EPC partners
  • Operational support — dedicated relationship managers, BI reporting, training webinars, and milestone management programs
  • Long-term partnership — not just a capital provider that disappears after funding

The question every EPC should be asking their finance partner:

"What value are you creating for me and my customers over the next 25 years — not just the next 25 days?"

The Bottom Line

Solar 2.0 companies treated customers as transactions and are now paying the price — bankruptcies, ABS downgrades, stock crashes, and regulatory actions. Solar 3.0 companies treat customers as long-term relationships and are being rewarded with the tightest ABS spreads, the strongest capital markets access, and the fastest-growing EPC networks in the industry.

The future of solar finance isn't about who can acquire the most customers. It's about who can serve them best over 25 years.
